Governor

7 candidates • Open seat (Brian Kemp term-limited)

Keisha Lance Bottoms

Former Atlanta Mayor, Biden Administration Senior Advisor

Polling: Leading at 38-43% but large undecided bloc means potential runoff

Key Positions:

  • Affordable healthcare through Medicaid expansion
  • Repealing Georgia's six-week abortion ban
  • Economic development and job creation
  • Voting rights protection

Background:

Atlanta mayor 2018-2022, attorney in private practice, Florida A&M and GSU Law graduate. National profile from handling 2020 protests and pandemic response.

Michael "Mike" Thurmond

Former DeKalb County CEO, State Labor Commissioner

Polling: Second place at 10-25% depending on poll

Key Positions:

  • Fighting for working families
  • Protecting and expanding healthcare access
  • Building education system with multiple pathways to success
  • Growing Georgia "faster, stronger, safer and more equitable"

Background:

Decades of public service: DeKalb County executive, state representative, labor commissioner, interim DeKalb school superintendent. Emphasis on practical governing experience.

Geoff Duncan

Former Republican Lt. Governor (switched parties)

Polling: Third place at 5-17%

Key Positions:

  • Argues he's the only Democrat who can win over moderates and Republicans
  • Rejected Trump's 2020 election fraud claims as Lt. Governor
  • Crossover appeal to suburban voters

Background:

Republican Lt. Governor who broke with Trump, switched parties in 2025. Markets himself as uniquely positioned to win swing voters but faces skepticism within Democratic base.

Jason Esteves

Former State Senator, Educator

Polling: Fourth place at 3-10%

Key Positions:

  • Lowering cost of living
  • Expanding healthcare access
  • Investing in small businesses
  • Increasing public education funding
  • Overturning Georgia's abortion ban

Background:

Former state senator and educator, focuses on cost-of-living and education issues. Appeals to working-class and education-focused constituencies.

Lieutenant Governor

3 candidates • Open seat (Burt Jones running for governor)

Josh McLaurin

State Senator, Attorney (Sandy Springs)

Key Positions:

  • Making healthcare more affordable by expanding Medicaid
  • Restricting corporate ownership of housing stock
  • Repealing Georgia's six-week abortion ban
  • Sees role as key Democratic messenger

Endorsements:

17 Senate colleagues have endorsed him

Background:

38-year-old attorney and state senator. Argues his legislative experience and colleague endorsements show he can work effectively with Republicans in the Senate.

Controversy: Drew criticism for saying Parkes and Wright "wouldn't be respected" by Republicans, which opponents called offensive given both are people of color.

Nabilah Parkes

Former State Senator, Consultant (Duluth)

Key Positions:

  • Expanding Medicaid
  • Lowering cost of living
  • Protecting voting rights
  • Fighting for working families

Background:

36-year-old consultant, daughter of Bangladeshi immigrants. Product of public schools and first-generation college graduate. Former Democratic Party operative who won election to Senate in 2022. Originally ran for insurance commissioner before switching to LG race late in process.

Controversy: Resigned from Senate seat with less than 10 legislative days left in 2026 session to focus on campaign, drawing criticism from McLaurin.

Richard N. Wright

CPA, Businessman

Key Positions:

  • Labels himself as moderate
  • Emphasizes business and legal experience
  • Could appeal to fiscally pragmatic Democrats and swing voters

Background:

CPA and businessman with less Capitol experience than McLaurin and Parkes. Positioning around business-backed messaging and moderate appeal.

Attorney General

2 candidates • Open seat (Chris Carr running for governor)

Tanya Miller

State Representative, House Democratic Caucus Chair

Key Positions:

  • Protecting civil rights and defending democracy
  • Targeting human trafficking, gang violence, illegal guns, fentanyl
  • Consumer protection against scams, predatory lending, price gouging
  • Supporting resources for local police
  • Maintaining independence and providing impartial legal guidance

Background:

State House member since 2023, House Democratic Caucus chair. Former assistant U.S. attorney in New York and Fulton County prosecutor specializing in homicides and crimes against women and children. Civil rights attorney background.

Bob Trammell

Attorney, Former House Minority Leader

Key Positions:

  • Running to be "The People's Attorney"
  • Protecting consumers and defending voting rights
  • Focusing on human trafficking, drugs, and gangs
  • Targeting fraud, financial schemes, online scams, data misuse
  • Emphasizes "free speech and fair elections are non-negotiable"

Background:

Served LaGrange-area House District 132 for six years, former minority leader. Lost reelection in 2020. Practices law in Luthersville. Promises consumer-focused approach prioritizing everyday Georgians over political agendas.

Secretary of State

4 candidates • Open seat (Brad Raffensperger running for governor)

Dana Barrett

Fulton County Commissioner

Key Positions:

  • Securing elections and protecting against Trump/MAGA takeover attempts
  • Streamlining professional licensing
  • Protecting seniors from scams and cyberattacks
  • Reinstating Secretary of State as chair of State Election Board

Background:

Fulton County Commissioner since 2023, former radio host. Outspoken Trump opponent who refused to seat Republican "extremists" on county election board. Was Democratic nominee for Congress in 2020.

Notable: One of two commissioners who voted against appointing Republican nominees to Fulton County Board of Elections, defying court order. Georgia Court of Appeals later sided with her.

Penny Brown Reynolds

Former Fulton County Judge, Biden Administration Official

Key Positions:

  • Safeguarding vulnerable communities from fraud
  • Modernizing professional licensing
  • Protecting voting rights and restoring trust in elections

Background:

Former Fulton County State Court judge for almost a decade. Former host of reality TV show "Family Court with Judge Penny." Served in Biden administration as Acting Assistant Secretary for Civil Rights at USDA.

Endorsements: Former Governor Roy Barnes

Adrian Consonery Jr.

Voting Rights Advocate (Marietta)

Key Positions:

  • Modernizing voter ID verification for absentee ballots
  • Launching voter awareness campaigns about registration and mail-in balloting
  • Promoting ranked-choice voting to save taxpayer dollars
  • Modernizing professional licensing processes

Background:

26-year-old community organizer and voting rights advocate. Never held elected office. Got involved after his own vote was challenged and nearly discarded in 2020. Positions himself as "change candidate" bringing fresh perspective.

Cam Ashling

Business Owner, Vietnamese Immigrant

Key Positions:

  • Modernizing the office and expediting licensing processes
  • Moving to hand-marked paper ballots counted by machines
  • Immigrant advocacy and AAPI community outreach

Background:

Political organizer, small-business owner, and licensed stockbroker. Left Vietnam as a child with her family. Never held elected office. Gwinnett County resident with long experience in civic engagement and voter outreach in AAPI community.

DeKalb County Commissioner District 7

3 candidates • No Republican opposition in November

LaDena Bolton (Incumbent)

County Commissioner, Chemist

Key Positions:

  • Opposes data centers in residential neighborhoods
  • Voted consistently to extend moratorium on data center applications
  • Focus on community input in decision-making
  • Infrastructure improvements across District 7

Background:

Incumbent who won special election runoff against Adams in December 2024. Born at Andrews Air Force Base, raised in DeKalb County. Chemist at Coca-Cola Company, Clark Atlanta University PhD. Three sons, active in community organizations.

Jacqueline Adams

Businesswoman, Georgia Alliance to End Homelessness

Key Positions:

  • Strong regulations on data centers - only in properly zoned industrial areas
  • Transparent water use requirements for data centers
  • Must provide real jobs, workforce training, fair return for residents
  • Focus on Candler Road and South Hairston area development

Background:

Businesswoman, part of Georgia Alliance to End Homelessness. Lost to Bolton in December 2024 special election runoff. Previously ran for state House. Emphasizes economic development and addressing homelessness.

Oneka Willabus

Entrepreneur

Key Positions:

  • "We have to start thinking about our residents when we make everyday decisions"
  • Holistic approach to District 7 needs - both north and south areas
  • Infrastructure focus to enable business development
  • Homeless services with fewer restrictions, better support

Background:

Entrepreneur emphasizing resident-centered decision making and comprehensive approach to district development and services.

Democratic Party Ballot Questions

These questions are non-binding and help the party understand voter priorities.

Statewide Democratic Question 1:

Should Georgia raise sales tax on everyday items to pay for an income tax cut benefiting millionaires and billionaires?

This question asks about tax policy priorities - whether to shift tax burden from high earners to everyday consumers.

Statewide Democratic Question 2:

Is it ethical for political candidates who have used public offices to enrich themselves to hold statewide office?

This question relates to ethics standards for public officials and self-enrichment while in office.

DeKalb County Democratic Questions:

1. Data Centers: Should DeKalb County require public hearings and strict environmental impact studies for all new data center developments?

2. ICE Cooperation: Do you support local law enforcement signing an agreement to act on behalf of ICE agents?

3. Mixed-Use Zoning: Should DeKalb County increase mixed-use zoning to create more walkable, affordable neighborhoods?

4. Gun Violence: Is gun violence reduction in Georgia one of your top concerns?
